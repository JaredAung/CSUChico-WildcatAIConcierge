# Downtown Chico — Student Guide

Source: https://www.downtownchico.com/ and local guides

## Overview

Downtown Chico sits just west of the Chico State campus, within easy walking and biking distance. It serves as a social hub for students and the community, featuring locally owned shops, restaurants, bars, coffee shops, and regular community events. Downtown feels like an extension of campus rather than a separate destination.

## Location and Accessibility

- Located immediately west of the Chico State campus
- Walking distance from most student housing
- Easily accessible by bike, skateboard, or on foot
- B-Line bus routes serve the downtown area (free with Wildcat ID)

## Broadway Street

Broadway is the main north-south street through downtown and connects most of the restaurants, bars, and shops students frequent. It's the primary meeting point for students heading out on Thursday, Friday, and Saturday nights.

## Food and Dining

Downtown offers food options at every price point:
- Quick meals between classes
- Late-night food after studying (many places stay open late)
- Casual Mexican, Asian, pizza, and brunch spots
- Sit-down restaurants that work for group dinners on a budget
- Food trucks (especially at Thursday Night Market)

### Notable Spots
- **Sierra Nevada Brewing Company** — One of Chico's most well-known landmarks. Popular for its relaxed outdoor setting, quality food, and a great place to bring visiting family. Located slightly farther from central downtown.
- **Madison Bear Garden** — A student and alumni staple since the 1970s, with walls layered in decades of Wildcat history and polaroids from generations of students.

## Coffee Shops and Study Spots

Downtown is loaded with coffee shops that double as study spaces. During midterms and finals, these places fill with students. Features students look for:
- Reliable Wi-Fi
- Enough seating to camp out
- Relaxed environment
- Affordable good coffee

## Thursday Night Market

Thursday Night Market is the most recognizable downtown event for Chico State students. During warmer months, downtown streets close to traffic and transform into a weekly community gathering:
- Food trucks and local vendors
- Handmade goods and art
- Live music and street performers
- Relaxed festival-style atmosphere

For many Wildcats, Thursday Night Market becomes part of their weekly rhythm — meet downtown after class, grab food, walk around, catch up with friends.

## Shopping

Downtown is home to many small, locally owned shops:
- Vintage and thrift clothing
- Gifts and décor for apartments
- Records, books, and art (including Melody Records)
- Outdoor and lifestyle gear
- One-of-a-kind local boutiques

Supporting local businesses is genuinely part of Chico's culture.

## Nightlife

Downtown nightlife is social, walkable, and student-oriented. Bars and music venues cluster together, so meeting friends doesn't require coordinating rides. Most students approach nights downtown casually — meet friends for food/drinks, walk between spots, and head home on foot.

## City Plaza

City Plaza is a hub of downtown activity, hosting art shows, culinary festivals, and community events. The downtown area retains its historic character while offering a vibrant cultural scene with art galleries, theaters, and live music venues.

## Living Near Downtown

### Advantages
- Walking to class, downtown, and Bidwell Park is all doable
- No car needed for daily life (saves money and hassle)
- Built-in social scene and convenience

### Considerations
- More noise and activity on weekends
- Street parking can be limited (ask about assigned parking)
- Visit apartments during both day and evening to assess noise levels

### Safety
Downtown Chico stays active and well-lit, especially near campus. Standard college-town safety awareness applies — stay aware at night, walk with friends when possible, know your route home.

# Bus and Transportation at Chico State

Source: https://www.csuchico.edu/sustainability/ride/bus.shtml

## Free Bus Pass — B-Line Transit Bus Service

Your Wildcat ID works as a free bus pass! Chico State has partnered with B-Line so students, staff, and faculty can ride the bus for free. Simply swipe your Chico State ID on the card scanner and you're good to go! With the bus, you can easily get to campus and all over town. Check out the bus routes and schedule at: https://www.blinetransit.com/bus-stop-location-maps. Visit the B-Line website at https://www.blinetransit.com/ for full route schedules and maps.

## Walking

As most housing is close to campus, it is easy to walk to and from the campus. It's also in the heart of downtown Chico, so it is easy to get to a restaurant or one-of-a-kind local shop.

## Biking, Skateboarding, and Scootering

There are a couple of paths you can take on campus. There is a path for riders between the east and west sides of campus located right next to the creek. Additionally, Warner St. provides access from North to South. Bike parking is everywhere on campus, and there are some newly installed skateboard/scooter racks.

## Car Rentals — Zipcar

The campus has two Zipcars on campus available for students to rent for a day trip or to help with moving, etc. Note: these Zipcars are the type that need to be returned to the Chico State campus and are not eligible to be returned to any other Zipcar location.

## Car and Parking

If you do decide to bring your car to campus, there is parking. Options include carpool parking passes, motorcycle passes, and EV parking passes among others. Visit the Parking Services website at https://www.csuchico.edu/parking/ for details.

## Free Bus Pass — B-Line Transit

Your Wildcat ID works as a free bus pass! Chico State has partnered with B-Line so students, staff, and faculty can ride for free. Simply swipe your Chico State ID on the card scanner and you're good to go! With the bus, you can easily get to campus and all over town for anything you need.

Check out the bus routes at: https://www.blinetransit.com/bus-stop-location-maps

### B-Line FAQ

**How to get on:** As the bus approaches the bus stop, stand near the bus stop sign and make sure the driver can see you. Some bus stops are served by more than one route so check the route number on the destination sign above the windshield. Once on, simply swipe your Wildcat ID card instead of paying a fare.

**How to get off:** When you see the bus nearing your destination, signal to the driver by pressing the yellow strip, pushing the yellow button, or pulling the cord above the window. The driver will stop at the next bus stop.

**How to store a bike:** Bike racks are located on the front of all B-Line buses. Racks are available on a first-come, first-served basis (usually 3 racks per bus). Tell the driver before you load your bike. When you get off, ask the driver to wait while you remove your bike from the rack.

**Where does it go?** Visit the B-Line website at https://www.blinetransit.com/ to check routes and schedules.

## University Housing Shuttle

University Housing provides a shuttle to transport students to and from campus. The shuttle makes stops at University Village (next to building 11), the Social, and the main campus (by Langdon Hall on W 1st St. and Ivy). The shuttle comes every 15 minutes and runs Monday through Friday.

More info: https://www.csuchico.edu/housing/uv-shuttle/index.shtml
